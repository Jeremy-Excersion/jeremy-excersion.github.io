About Taniti

Welcome to the island of Taniti, a tropical paradise located in the Pacific. It spans less than 500 square miles with enchanting views, diverse landscape - everything from sandy and rocky beaches to breathtaking rainforests. Home to a community of approximately 20,000 locals. The Taniti economy mostly consisted of fishing and agriculture, until recent years, when tourism to the island started becoming more popular.

Frequently Asked Questions

Power Outlets

Power outlets on Taniti are 120 volts, the same as in the United States.

Alcohol Regulations

Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m.

Drinking Age

The drinking age on Taniti is 18, although it is not strictly enforced.

Language

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. However, very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by older residents.

Medical Facilities

Taniti has one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.

Safety

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti, but as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes.

National Holidays

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays. Many tourist attractions and restaurants may be closed on holidays, so visitors should plan accordingly.

Currency and Payment

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency, but many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.
